The purpose of this post is just for me to remind of those equations and techniques back for no particular reason.

Calculation of Jacobian and Dealiasing

To calculate the Fourier transform of the products appearing in the Jacobian term, we use the standard procedure:

  1. apply the `3/2’ rule to pad zeros around the Fourier coefficients of each variables; this is to avoid the aliasing effect (wiki) arising from a product of two variables.
  2. apply the inverse transform to each variable to go back to the phyiscal domain.
  3. point-wise multiplication.
  4. apply the forward transform.
  5. extract the 2/3 (in each axis) of the result to align to the original resolution.

An (bad) alternative would be to apply an convolution, which is a slow N2N^2N2 operation, while the above procedure is about Nln⁡(N)N \ln(N)Nln(N).
