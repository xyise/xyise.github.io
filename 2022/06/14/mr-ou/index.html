Ornstein-Uhlenbeck Process

The Ornstein-Uhlenbeck (OU) process is one of the simplest mean-reverting process with many applications including modelling financial time series such as interest rates (known as the Vasicek model in such a context).

Unfortunately, this method is unstable as it can (and does in this example) produce negative mean-reversion rate θ\thetaθ, making the process mean-diverting! For example, from the middle figure, negative estimated values of θ\thetaθ are observed in early 2020 (Covid), 2008 (Financial Crisis), and most previous crisis periods. When θ\thetaθ becomes negative, the estimated means μ\muμ start fluctuated unstably (top figure), making the model unusable.

Momentum Matching - Stable Alternative
