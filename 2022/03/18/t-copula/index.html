The purpose of this post (and a notebook) is to illustrate t-copula versus Gaussian copula.

Gaussian and Student-t Marginals/Copulas

Four combinations to consider:

  • Gaussian marginals with Gaussian copula
  • Gaussian marginals with Student-t copula
  • Student-t marginals with Gaussian copula
  • Student-t marginals with Student-t copula

Bivariate distributions with linear correlation ρ=0.5\rho = 0.5ρ=0.5 are used.
For Student-t, the degree of freedom, denoted by ν\nuν, is set to 3.

Correlation measurement

Here, the following correlation measures are considered:

  • ρp\rho_pρp​: Pearson correlation, also known as linear correlation
  • ρs\rho_sρs​: Spearman correlation, a well-known rank correlation measure
  • ρk\rho_kρk​: Kendall correlation, another well-known rank correlation measure

conversions

For bivariate normal (x,y)(x,y)(x,y), we have

  • ρp=2sin⁡(πρs/6)\rho_p = 2 \sin(\pi \rho_s / 6)ρp​=2sin(πρs​/6)
  • ρp=sin⁡(πρk/2)\rho_p = \sin(\pi\rho_k / 2)ρp​=sin(πρk​/2)
    See, for example, http://www.columbia.edu/~mh2078/QRM/Copulas.pdf

The second equation for Kendall correlation holds for elliptic distributions including Student-t. See https://people.kth.se/~lindskog/papers/KendallsTau.pdf.

Student-t

Note that a multivariate Student-t distribution YYY with ν\nuν degree of freedom can be generated through
Y=XQ/νY = \frac{X}{\sqrt{Q/\nu}}Y=Q/ν​X​
where XXX is a multivariate normal with variance matrix Σ\SigmaΣ and QQQ is a χ2\chi^2χ2 distribution with ν\nuν degree of freedom. The variace matrix for YYY is given by
νν−2Σifν>2\frac{\nu}{\nu-2} \Sigma \quad \textrm{if} \quad \nu > 2ν−2ν​Σifν>2
Otherwise, undefined. See https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multivariate_t-distribution. Consequently, the correlation matrix for YYY is equal to that of XXX.
